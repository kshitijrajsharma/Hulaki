Hulaki Child Safety Standards

Effective date: 12 July 2026

Hulaki is a field mapping app with group chat. These are its standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE).

Zero tolerance

Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any content or conduct that sexualises, grooms, exploits, or endangers a child is prohibited in Hulaki. This applies to messages, photos, group names, and every other part of the app. There is no exception and no warning: accounts and groups used this way are removed.

Reporting

Report CSAE content or conduct to krschap@proton.me with the subject line "CSAE report". Include the group name and invite link, what you saw, and when. Hulaki is maintained by one developer. Reports are read by that developer, and CSAE reports are handled ahead of everything else.

If a child is in immediate danger, contact your local police first.

What the operator can and cannot see

Messages and photos in Hulaki are end to end encrypted. The server holds only ciphertext, so the operator cannot read message content and cannot scan it for CSAM. Content is readable only by the members of a group, through the group key that is shared in the invite link.

Two things are readable by the operator. A group listed in the public directory publishes its name, photo, and approximate area. Reports sent by email include whatever the reporter chooses to send. Both are acted on.

What happens after a report

A public group listing that violates these standards is deleted from the directory, which stops it being discoverable and joinable. Where a reporter supplies the group invite link, the group's encrypted data is deleted from the server. CSAM is reported to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) and, where required, to the authorities in Nepal. Evidence is preserved as the law requires.

In the app

Groups are private by default and are joined only through an invite link or an admin's approval. A group admin can remove any member from the group and can delete the group.

Point of contact

Child safety point of contact: krschap@proton.me.

Legal compliance

Hulaki complies with child safety laws that apply to it, and with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy. These standards are reviewed as the app changes.
